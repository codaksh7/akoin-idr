# IDR Logo Design Note

## Design Choices

**Colour:** The IDR logo uses orange (#FF6B00) as the primary mark colour against black/dark grey faces. Orange conveys urgency, visibility and energy — all apt for a risk-focused organisation. White background compatibility ensures the logo reads clearly across both dark digital interfaces and light print contexts.

**Typography:** The wordmark uses "Outfit" — a geometric sans-serif with clean, neutral weight distribution that reads well at large display sizes and small favicon/header sizes alike. The abbreviation "IDR" is set large and bold in orange; the full name "Institute of Digital Risk" is set at a smaller scale in a lighter grey, creating a clear visual hierarchy.

**Symbolism:** The core mark is a three-dimensional cube rendered in isometric wireframe/flat-face style. The cube evokes structure, resilience and reliable containment — all core values of risk management. The angled perspective suggests forward motion and strategic thinking. A smaller "accent cube" is positioned on the top-right of the primary cube, suggesting layered risk modelling and the AI/innovation dimension of IDR's work. This deliberate compositional asymmetry adds dynamism without sacrificing the clean, minimalist tech/education feel required by the brief.

**Two Variants:**
- `logo-icon.svg` — The cube mark only, usable as a favicon, app icon or compact mobile header logo.
- `logo-full.svg` — The cube mark with the full wordmark, for desktop headers, letterheads and presentations.
